🌊 Local Tips

  • Crescent Beach and the Point of Rocks area (right near you) is the quieter, more local end of the island. Siesta Public Beach (Village side, 2.4 mi) is the famous white-sand stretch with facilities — get there before 9 AM in July to claim a spot.
  • The Siesta Key Trolley runs the length of the island and is free. Great way to get to the Village without hunting for parking.
  • For 4th of July (Sat 7/4), fireworks launch at ~9:15 PM from Siesta Public Beach — visible from anywhere along Crescent Beach, meaning you can walk from the rental. 20,000–30,000 people attend; get your beach spot by 7:30 PM.
  • Kayaking/paddleboarding is best on the bay side (Midnight Pass, Stickney Point) — much calmer water than the Gulf, and that's where all the wildlife is.
  • Always confirm hours before going — July 4th weekend some spots change hours or close early.

🎆 35th Annual Siesta Key Fireworks — America's 250th Birthday

Fireworks launch at approximately 9:15 PM from just north of the volleyball courts at Siesta Public Beach. Viewable from anywhere along Crescent Beach — including a short walk from your rental. 20,000–30,000 people attend. Stake your beach spot by 7:30 PM. Do not bring personal fireworks. The event runs 8:00–9:30 PM.

🎪 Star-Spangled Spectacular at Waterside Place

A massive family-friendly 4th of July celebration in Lakewood Ranch. Sat July 4, 6–9 PM. Jaw-dropping circus acts, carnival games, kids' zone, local food vendors, and a grand finale drone show at 9 PM. Magic & mentalism show at 7 PM, Circus Arts Conservatory at 8 PM. Free to attend, bring a lawn chair. Complimentary trolley runs 5:30–10 PM. If you want an alternative to the beach fireworks (or want to start there and drive back for the Siesta Key fireworks), this is the event.
